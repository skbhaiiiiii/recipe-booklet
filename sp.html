Shahi Paneer 

Shahi paneer or Paneer Rajwadi[1] or Chhena Rajwadi or Raajsi Chhena is a preparation of chhena or paneer, native to the Indian subcontinent, consisting of a thick gravy 
of cream, tomatoes and Indian spices.This dish is prepared by emulsifying tomatoes, onions, ground cashews, clarified butter and cream into a curry, with the addition of 
chhena/paneer cubes and a variety of spices

Recipe

Ingredients for Shahi Paneer:

  1. ½ cup onions – chopped
  2. 2 tablespoons cashews
  3. 1 tablespoon almonds
  4. 1 tablespoon melon seeds – without the skins (magaz)
  5. ½ teaspoon garlic – chopped, or 3 to 4 small to medium-sized garlic
  6. ½ teaspoon chopped ginger – chopped or 1 inch ginger
  7. 1.5 cups water
  8. 3 tablespoons Ghee (clarified butter) or oil or butter
  9. 1 teaspoon Coriander Powder (ground coriander)
  10. ¼ teaspoon Garam Masala
  11. ½ teaspoon red chili powder or cayenne pepper
  12. ½ cup Curd (yogurt), 125 grams – whisked till smooth
  13. 1 to 1.5 cups water or add as needed
  14. ½ to 1 teaspoon sugar or add as required
  15. ▢salt as required
  16. ¼ teaspoon cardamom powder
  17. 15 to 20 strands saffron – crushed lightly
  18. 200 grams Paneer (Indian cottage cheese)
  19. 1 tablespoon heavy cream or add as required – optional
  20. 1 teaspoon kewra water (screwpine water) or rose water

Step by Step Recipe of Shahi Paneer

        1.Take the nuts and seeds in a bowl and rinse them a couple of times with water. Drain all the water and set aside.
        2.Cook onions, cashews, almonds, magaz or melon seeds, ginger and garlic in 1.5 cups water for 8 to 10 minutes until the onions soften.
        3.Strain some of the stock (water in which the onions, nuts etc were cooked) and set it aside. Let the onions cool at room temperature.
        4.Make a fine paste of the cooked onions, ginger, garlic along with the nuts and melon seeds, adding 2 to 4 tablespoons of the strained stock.
        5.Whisk fresh curd (yogurt) till smooth and set aside.
        6.Heat ghee in a frying pan on medium-low to medium heat.
        7.Fry all the whole spices (listed in the above ingredients) until they release their aroma in the ghee.
        8.Be very attentive here, as spices are delicate and can burn quickly if you aren’t paying attention.
        9.Add the ground onion paste and mix very well.
        10.On a low heat sauté stirring non-stop for 7 to 8 minutes or until the fat starts to leave the sides of the paste.
        11.Add red chilli powder or cayenne pepper, garam masala powder and coriander powder. Mix well.
        12.Reduce heat to a low or turn off the heat.
        13.Add the whisked yogurt, water, sugar and salt as required.
        14.Mix very well and simmer gravy for 10 to 12 minutes until it thickens.
        15.Add the cardamom powder and crushed saffron.
        16.Stir and add the cubed paneer and cream.
        17.Mix gently and turn off heat.
        18.Add the kewra water and stir. You can even add rose water.
        19.Garnish Shahi Paneer with some coriander leaves or mint leaves or a few saffron strands. Serve the Shahi Paneer with cumin rice or roti, naan or paratha
